✓ What your project should steal

Title-only collapsed state

Articles show just the headline until clicked. Zero cognitive overhead on scan. The user controls depth. This is the right default — your current design shows all 3 bullets immediately, which creates visual fatigue when scanning 20+ items.

Inline expansion (no navigation, no modal)

Clicking expands in-place. The feed stays in context — you never lose your scroll position. Keeps the mental model simple.

Source name always visible

Before reading anything, you know where it came from. Trust signal before commitment.

✗ What your project should reject

The significance score (1.8–10 slider)

Forces a meta-judgement — "is this significant enough to read?" — before you've even seen the headline. You've already solved this better: your curation (Ars, TechCrunch, Verge + builder tweets + WeChat) IS the quality filter. No score needed.

Too many category tabs

9 categories creates a navigation burden. Your content is already meaningfully segmented by source type — EN/ZH toggle + source label does more work with less UI. The category bar also eats vertical space on every load.

Key design insight from this site:

Progressive disclosure is the right UX model. Show title → click to reveal summary + questions → click question to stream answer. Three levels of depth, each earned by a deliberate user action. The current design skips level 1 (collapses directly to level 2 open state).
